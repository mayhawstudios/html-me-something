My Trip to China 2016

Introduction

In college, I didn't have any options to study abroad. One of my majors didn't have any places to travel, and the other one did, but I didn't have much money at the time and when an opportunity did come up, I was already in my last semester before graduating. When I finally found a job and started my career, one of my sisters was planning on getting married while the other one got a chance to go abroad to China. I thought that my chance to do something exciting and new had passed me by, but then I was told I could pay to be a chapperone and I lept at the chance! After several months of payment, passport and visa applications, I finally got to go someplace amazing!

Here are just some of the places I got to see in China:

Great Wall of China

There are actually multiple sections of the wall to visit, but we went to just one of the parts. And on this part of the wall, there are two paths to the top. One gently slopes and the other is harder to scale. I chose the harder route. Over thousands of years and tourists (Chinese and foreign, to some more rural parts of China, Beijing is considered a huge tourist site) the steps have worn down and not evenly. So some steps were barely taller than the previous ones, and some were huge! I made it to the top eventually, but going down was much harder because of how big the slope was.

Chinese Tea Ceremony

I was more of a tea drinker than a coffee drinker before moving to China, but after drinking jasmine tea with every meal and a ceremonial tea ceremony got me even more into tea! In a traditional tea ceremony, the tea is quickly rinsed with hot water to remove into any impurities and open the tea up, then the water is poured through a slotted bamboo table into a bin below. Then the tea is steeped in a kettle and poured into glasses. The long thin glass is for smelling the tea, then the tea is poured into the bowl-shaped tea cup for drinking.

We tried a variety of teas such as:

  • Ginsing Oolong
  • Jasmine Pearls
  • Black Tea with Rosebuds
  • Dried Fruit Tea (Tisane)
  • Pu'erh (Aged black tea)
